25 Under 25: Pre-Playoff Edition

Updated: Apr. 8, 2023
# Progress Team Player P Summary
1 Jayson Tatum SF/PF Taking the top spot currently, Jayson's game has taken another leap following his finals appearance. He's a complete 3-level scorer and is looking to lead his team through a deep playoff run once again.
2 Luka Doncic PG/SF Taking a small step back, although his stats have been phenomenomal once again for the shot-creating savant, questions have been pondered about his winning habits and defense this year.
3 Shai Gilgeous-Alexander PG The biggest leaper on the board. SGA has blossomed into a 30PPG scorer, and proved himself to be inevitable driving to the rim and getting to the free throw line. True leader of his young thunder squad and has the team ahead of schedule in playing winning basketball.
4 Ja Morant PG Admist a controversial year, Ja remains a 1 of 1 player in today's NBA. He gets to the rim when he wants, and makes explosive finishes over centers look routine. His playmaking has steadily improved since coming into the league and the electric PG looks to run the west for years to come.
5 De'Aaron Fox PG One of the most clutch players in the league has found a perfect balace in his scoring & playmaking abilites. The lightning-quick pg has lead his King's to a season no one could have predicted, clinching a top 3 seed in the west.
6 Trae Young PG Trae has been a tricky case to say the least. His playmaking and shooting abilities continue to shine in the statistical categories. However, the defense and body language has become a real problem, as well as issues with how coachable Trae can be. It might be time for a shake-up in the Atlanta roster soon.
7 - Anthony Edwards SG/SF Ant has been great, and showed much improvement taking upon a leadership role for the Wolves this year. His decision-making has improved but with the lanes clogged in Minny, we haven't seen Ant take the leap some expected this year.
8 Darius Garland PG The always steady young PG has had a less is more season. With the addition of Mitchell to his Cavs squad, DG has been able to focus more on setting up his teammates and has been very efficient in doing so. Seeing him being able to play next to another star guard is a great sign for things to come.
9 Jaren Jackson Jr. PF A big leaper this year, JJJ has put forth a truly amazing DPOY campaign. His rim defending has reached another level as he's blossomed into one of the best shot blockers in the league. Pairing this with his improved 3pt shooting, JJJ has a bright future ahead.
10 - Tyrese Haliburton PG Shot creation and playmaking remain to be his bread & butter. Although battling through injuries this year, Haliburton has done enough to prove he will be one of the best point guards for years to come.
11 - Evan Mobley PF/C The lanky forward has been extremely efficient and does a little bit of everything for the promising Cavs squad. Although, the scoring has yet to evolve, the defensive skills, rebounding and fluidity of Mobley are flourishing, and he will be a key to the Cavs long-term success going forward.
12 Zion Williamson PF This may seem low but injuries have become a real concern for the generational talent. He has yet to prove he can play a full healthy season, and with such a unique body type, it's hard to contextulize the concerns many have. Zion is still a 1 of 1 player, and should he get right this summer, can expect to skyrocket our rankings.
13 Lamelo Ball PG Ball has everything you want in a modern day point guard. His feel for the game is unmatched, and his shooting has steadily improved with more volume. Unfortunately, Lamelo has struggle with staying healthy this year, and we all hope the health concerns that affect his brother do not repeat a pattern for the young savant.
14 Anfernee Simons SG/PG Portland has found their successor. Simons has been a bright spot for a dissapointing Portland squad. His shooting is precise, he has size, quickness and elite touch on his floater. He has proven he can play on and off the ball, and with Dame likely on his way out at some point, Anfernee can expect his role to continue to grow.
15 - Tyrese Maxey SG/PG The quick & explosive guard might be Philadelphia's 2nd most consistent player. Maxey gets to the rim with ease, and can get hot in a hurray. He plays winning basketball, and makes winning plays for a winning team. He may not have as big a role as some of the players ahead of him, but he can get buckets at will, and will be crucial for the 76ers title run this year.
16 Paolo Banchero PF/SF The ROTY has had a stellar inaugural season. He's everything we expected and more. Paolo is an advanced super-sized wing scorer, and has only scratched the surface of his potential. The Magic are still growing, but have shown glimpses of being able to compete against the best this season with Paolo leading the charge.
17 Jordan Poole SG JP is a peculiar case. His creative ball-handling and shot-making abilities are undeniable, but his decision-making and IQ have left a lot to be desired this year. Their seems to be some uneasiness in the Warriors organization with handing the reins off to JP. The talent is indefinetely there, and it's not the easiest situation for a young player to be in, but we need to see more consistent play from JP for him to traject further.
18 - Franz Wagner PF A smooth player, Franz is a swiss army-knife who will be a corner-stone for the Magic for years to come. He shined in the FIBA Europes last summer suiting up for Germany. Franz is the real deal, and can be a all-star within 2-3 years if he continues to develop.
19 Nic Claxton C A shocker to some, we see Claxton for what he is, one of the best young centers in the league. He protects the rim every night, brings intensity, and finishes insanely well (70% fg). Clax is a dynamic big man with lots of room to grow.
20 Scottie Barnes SF/PF A slightly dissapointing year for the former ROTY. Scottie's taken a small stepback in most areas, and needs to show he can continue to develop going forward.
21 - Desmond Bane SG Memphis' sniper guard has remained rock steady. He might not have as high a ceiling as some others on this list, but he's exactly what the Grizz need right now.
22 Jalen Williams SF/SG The biggest suprise of the year. The two-way player has had an amazing campaign for the young Thunder. He gets after it on defense, and gets to the rim with strong finishes consistently. Shai and him are heading to the play-in and OKC can feel great about their young core.
23 Trey Murphy III SF The 6'9 Wing is a special 3&D player. He's the kind of player who can fit in with any team in ths league. He's taken a huge leap this year, increasing his drives to the rim. He can shoot, he can defend and he can dunk the basketball. TMIII is one of the best under-the-rader players in the NBA.
24 - Tyler Herro SG Not great this year, but still good enough to crack our list. Herro has had big playoff moments and has unique shot-creating skills.
25 Jaden Mcdaniels SF By far, the best defender on this list, and one of the best and most versatille defenders in the league. Mcdaniels plays winning basketball, and knows his role. The shooting is still a work in progress, but has showed promise and improvement.
